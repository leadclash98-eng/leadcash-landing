# 10 Tasks Your Virtual Assistant Should Handle for B2B Growth

Most B2B founders and sales leaders know they need help. The to-do list is endless, and the tasks that drive growth -- prospecting, outreach, data management, content -- keep getting pushed aside by the urgent demands of running the business.

A virtual assistant can change that equation. But the key is knowing exactly what to delegate. Handing off the wrong tasks creates more work (in oversight and corrections), while handing off the right ones creates immediate leverage.

This guide covers 10 specific, high-impact tasks that a B2B virtual assistant should be handling for your business. Each one is practical, clearly defined, and directly tied to revenue growth or operational efficiency.

## 1. Lead Research and Prospect List Building

**Why it matters:** Your sales pipeline starts with finding the right people to talk to. Without a steady flow of qualified prospects, growth stalls.

**What your VA should do:**

- Use LinkedIn, Sales Navigator, Apollo, or ZoomInfo to identify prospects matching your ICP
- Compile prospect lists with complete data: name, title, company, industry, email, LinkedIn URL, and personalization hooks
- Verify email addresses using tools like Hunter, NeverBounce, or ZeroBounce
- Research each prospect for conversation starters: recent LinkedIn posts, company news, funding announcements, job changes
- Organize lists by segment, priority, or campaign assignment

**Expected output:** A well-trained VA can research and compile 50-100 qualified prospects per day, depending on the depth of research required.

**Tip:** Create a prospect research template with required fields so your VA delivers consistent, usable data every time.

## 2. CRM Data Entry and Maintenance

**Why it matters:** A clean CRM is the foundation of effective sales operations. Dirty data leads to missed follow-ups, inaccurate forecasts, and frustrated reps.

**What your VA should do:**

- Enter new contacts, companies, and deals into your CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, or others)
- Update contact information when people change jobs, get promoted, or move companies
- Log call notes, meeting summaries, and email interactions
- Merge duplicate records and clean up inconsistencies
- Update deal stages based on feedback from account executives
- Run weekly data quality checks and flag issues

**Expected output:** All CRM records stay current, duplicates are caught within 24 hours, and your pipeline reports reflect reality.

**Tip:** Set up a weekly CRM audit checklist for your VA to run through every Friday. This prevents data decay from accumulating.

## 3. LinkedIn Outreach and Engagement

**Why it matters:** LinkedIn is the primary channel for B2B prospecting. Consistent outreach and engagement build visibility, trust, and pipeline.

**What your VA should do:**

- Send personalized connection requests from approved templates
- Execute follow-up message sequences after connections accept
- Engage with prospects' content (like, comment, share) to build familiarity
- Monitor incoming messages and route conversations appropriately
- Track connection acceptance rates, response rates, and meetings booked
- Manage multiple LinkedIn profiles if running campaigns across your team

**Expected output:** 50-100 new connection requests per day, consistent follow-up cadence, and a steady stream of conversations feeding into your pipeline.

**Tip:** Give your VA three to five different connection request templates and rotate them weekly to avoid LinkedIn flagging repetitive behavior.

## 4. Email Campaign Management

**Why it matters:** Email remains one of the most effective B2B outreach channels. But managing sequences, tracking replies, and following up consistently is time-consuming.

**What your VA should do:**

- Set up and manage email sequences in tools like Lemlist, Mailshake, Woodpecker, or HubSpot
- Personalize email templates for each batch of recipients
- Monitor deliverability metrics (open rates, bounce rates, spam complaints)
- Handle replies: categorize as interested, not interested, or out of office
- Schedule follow-ups for interested prospects
- A/B test subject lines and message copy
- Maintain email lists and suppress unsubscribes

**Expected output:** Consistent outbound email volume with personalization, prompt reply handling, and clean deliverability metrics.

**Tip:** Have your VA flag any reply that includes a question or objection they are not sure how to handle. Better to escalate than to send a poor response.

## 5. Calendar and Meeting Management

**Why it matters:** Every scheduling hiccup is a potential lost deal. Smooth calendar management keeps your sales process running and your close rate high.

**What your VA should do:**

- Schedule meetings between prospects and account executives
- Send calendar invitations with proper details (agenda, video link, preparation materials)
- Send meeting reminders 24 hours and 1 hour before scheduled calls
- Reschedule meetings when conflicts arise
- Follow up with no-shows to rebook
- Block focus time and prep time around important meetings
- Maintain a master calendar view across the sales team

**Expected output:** Zero scheduling conflicts, reduced no-show rates, and more time for your team to prepare for and execute meetings.

**Tip:** Use a scheduling tool like Calendly or HubSpot Meetings to reduce back-and-forth. Your VA can send the link and handle any edge cases manually.

## 6. Sales Reporting and Analytics

**Why it matters:** Data-driven decisions require accurate, timely reports. But compiling data from multiple sources is tedious work that does not require strategic thinking.

**What your VA should do:**

- Pull data from your CRM, LinkedIn, email tools, and other sources
- Compile weekly and monthly sales activity reports
- Create pipeline snapshots showing deals by stage, value, and age
- Track individual rep performance against KPIs
- Build charts and dashboards in Google Sheets, Excel, or your CRM's reporting tools
- Prepare data for sales meetings and leadership reviews
- Flag notable trends or anomalies

**Expected output:** Consistent, accurate reports delivered on schedule, giving you and your team a clear picture of performance without the manual data gathering.

**Tip:** Create report templates once, then have your VA update them on a recurring schedule. The first report takes time to build; subsequent ones are quick updates.

## 7. Social Media Content Scheduling

**Why it matters:** A consistent social media presence builds credibility and keeps you visible to your target audience. But posting regularly requires planning and discipline.

**What your VA should do:**

- Schedule LinkedIn posts, articles, and updates using tools like Buffer, Hootsuite, or LinkedIn's native scheduler
- Repurpose existing content (blog posts, webinars, case studies) into social media format
- Curate relevant industry content to share alongside your original posts
- Monitor engagement (likes, comments, shares) and flag notable interactions
- Respond to comments on your posts (using approved tone and messaging)
- Track social media performance metrics weekly

**Expected output:** Consistent posting schedule (3-5 times per week on LinkedIn), growing engagement metrics, and increased profile visibility.

**Tip:** Batch content creation monthly. Spend one session with your VA outlining 20 post ideas, then let them draft and schedule the content throughout the month.

## 8. Competitor and Market Research

**Why it matters:** Knowing what your competitors are doing and how the market is evolving helps you position more effectively and spot opportunities.

**What your VA should do:**

- Monitor competitor LinkedIn profiles, websites, and content
- Track competitor product launches, pricing changes, and messaging updates
- Compile competitive intelligence briefs on a monthly or quarterly basis
- Set up Google Alerts and social listening for competitor mentions
- Research industry trends, new tools, and emerging best practices
- Identify new market segments or use cases based on research findings

**Expected output:** A monthly competitive intelligence report that keeps your team informed without anyone spending hours doing the research themselves.

**Tip:** Give your VA a list of 5-10 competitors and specific areas to track (pricing, features, messaging, hiring patterns). Focused research produces more useful insights than broad monitoring.

## 9. Event and Webinar Support

**Why it matters:** Events and webinars generate leads, build relationships, and establish thought leadership. But the logistics can be overwhelming.

**What your VA should do:**

- Research relevant industry events, conferences, and webinars to attend or sponsor
- Handle event registration and logistics
- Manage webinar setup in tools like Zoom, GoToWebinar, or Demio
- Create and send invitation emails to your prospect and customer lists
- Track RSVPs and send reminders to registered attendees
- Compile attendee lists after events and add them to your CRM
- Send post-event follow-up emails
- Prepare event recap summaries

**Expected output:** Smooth event execution, maximum attendance, and every attendee captured in your CRM for follow-up.

**Tip:** Create a standard event checklist that covers pre-event, day-of, and post-event tasks. This ensures nothing falls through the cracks regardless of the event type.

## 10. Document and Proposal Preparation

**Why it matters:** Sales materials -- proposals, one-pagers, case studies, pitch decks -- are essential to the sales process. But creating and customizing them for each prospect is time-intensive.

**What your VA should do:**

- Customize proposal templates for specific prospects (inserting company name, relevant case studies, tailored pricing)
- Format and proofread sales documents
- Create one-pagers and leave-behind materials
- Compile case studies from raw data and customer interviews
- Update pitch decks with current data and examples
- Maintain a library of sales collateral organized by use case and audience
- Ensure all materials are branded consistently

**Expected output:** Professional, customized sales materials delivered on time, with your team spending zero time on formatting and production.

**Tip:** Build a master template library with your VA. Once the templates are solid, customization takes minutes rather than hours.

## How to Prioritize: Where to Start

If you are new to working with a virtual assistant, trying to delegate all 10 tasks at once is a recipe for overwhelm -- for you and the VA. Start with the tasks that will have the biggest immediate impact.

### Start Here (Week 1-2)

- **Lead research and list building:** Gets your pipeline moving immediately
- **CRM data entry:** Cleans up your existing foundation

### Add Next (Week 3-4)

- **LinkedIn outreach and engagement:** Starts converting your research into conversations
- **Calendar and meeting management:** Removes friction from booking calls

### Layer In (Month 2)

- **Email campaign management:** Adds a second outreach channel
- **Sales reporting:** Gives you visibility into performance

### Scale Up (Month 3+)

- **Social media scheduling:** Builds your long-term presence
- **Competitor research:** Sharpens your positioning
- **Event support:** Taps into high-value lead sources
- **Document preparation:** Speeds up your sales cycle
